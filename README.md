# Order-Invariant Identification in SV-BVAR Models: Uncertainty, Financial Volatility, and GDP tail risk

Author: Manuel Gloria

## Abstract
Policy uncertainty and financial market stress jointly shape the distribution of future GDP growth, yet their interaction is poorly understood. Using a Bayesian
VAR with stochastic volatility (SV-BVAR) estimated on UK data, this paper documents an asymmetric amplification mechanism: financial stress shocks are substantially
more recessionary when policy uncertainty is high, whereas uncertainty shocks are not systematically more contractionary when financial stress is elevated.
This asymmetry is clearly visible under order-invariant identification but largely muted under standard recursive Cholesky schemes. A 2025 counterfactual exercise confirms the policy relevance of this finding: persistent financial stress worsens GDP-at-Risk roughly two to three times more than the GDP median, indicating that tail risk can be materially understated when analysis focuses on central projections alone. Historical narrative checks and out-of-sample forecasting experiments provide additional support; forecasting performance remains broadly comparable across identification schemes.
